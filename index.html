Deduction Methods Explained
OSD vs. Itemized Deductions — Which Is Better for You?Optional Standard Deduction · NIRC Sec. 34(L) · No receipts needed for OSD
▾
Key ruleThe OSD is elected on the first quarterly return (1701Q Q1) and is irrevocable for the year. You cannot switch mid-year.
A
Optional Standard Deduction (OSD) — 40% of gross
Who benefits: Taxpayers whose actual expenses are below 40% of gross revenue. Professionals with mostly time-based fees (consultants, lawyers, accountants). Anyone who doesn't keep complete receipts.
Advantage: No documentation burden — the 40% is automatic. File Form 1701A instead of 1701.
Drawback: If actual expenses exceed 40%, you leave deductions on the table.
B
Itemized Deductions — Sec. 34(A) through (M)
Who benefits: Taxpayers with actual expenses exceeding 40–50% of gross revenue. Businesses with significant COGS, payroll, rent, or equipment costs.
Advantage: Deduct all real, documented expenses. Better when expense ratio is high.
Drawback: Requires complete receipts and records all year. May trigger BIR audit. File Form 1701 (not 1701A). Gross > ₱3M requires Audited Financial Statements.
Breakeven rule of thumbOSD wins when actual expenses < 40% of gross. Itemized wins when actual expenses > 40–50% of gross (accounting for the 3% percentage tax which applies to both). Use the Regime Calculator tab for a precise side-by-side comparison.
Income Type Guides
Rental Income Guide — RA 9653, VAT Treatment & Sworn DeclarationRent Control Act · BIR Revenue Regulations · Common landlord compliance checklist
▾
1
Determine if RA 9653 (Rent Control) applies
Covered: Residential units with monthly rent ≤ ₱10,000 in Metro Manila; ≤ ₱5,000 in other areas (as of the most recent IRR). Effect: Annual rent increase capped at 7%. You cannot legally charge above this cap — but you must still report and pay tax on actual rent received.
Not covered: Commercial units and residential units above the ceiling rent are not subject to RA 9653.
2
Register with BIR and determine your tax treatment
If total annual gross receipts ≤ ₱3M (non-VAT): File quarterly percentage tax (2551Q) at 3% + income tax (1701Q / 1701A). If gross receipts > ₱3M: Must register for VAT. Charge 12% VAT on rent, file 2550M/2550Q.
Note: EWT on rent is 5% (ATC: WC120). Your tenant (if a corporation or top withholding agent) must withhold 5% of rent and issue you a BIR Form 2307.
3
Issue Official Receipts and maintain records
Issue a BIR-registered OR for every monthly rental payment. Keep a rent ledger per unit. CAS or manual books accepted. If you have 5+ units or mixed rental/other income, consider a separate BIR registration per business address.
4
File Sworn Declaration if requesting lower EWT
If your gross receipts are < ₱3M and you want your tenants to withhold at a lower rate, file a Sworn Declaration (BIR prescribed form) on or before January 15 each year. Submit to each withholding agent (tenant). Without this, corporate tenants will withhold at the standard rate.
Deadline: January 15 of every taxable year. Failure to file means the payer must withhold at the higher EWT rate.
5
Include rental income in quarterly and annual ITR
Rental income is reported as business income on 1701Q (quarterly) and 1701A or 1701 (annual). Creditable withholding (from Form 2307) is applied against income tax due. Excess CWT may be refunded or carried over.
Freelancer & Professional Tax Flow — Gross Receipts → EWT → Net Tax DueSelf-employed professionals · NIRC Sec. 24(A) · BIR Form 1701Q
▾
1
Receive payment — client withholds EWT and issues Form 2307
When a corporate client or top withholding agent pays you, they deduct EWT before releasing your fee. Standard rate: 10% (regular professional). Rate drops to 5% if you file a sworn declaration of income below ₱3M.
They issue BIR Form 2307 quarterly. Collect every 2307 — each one is a tax credit.
2
File quarterly income tax return (1701Q) — cumulative method
Each 1701Q reports year-to-date gross receipts, not just the quarter's. Q2 reports Q1+Q2, Q3 reports Q1+Q2+Q3.
Tax computation: Apply 8% or graduated rate to cumulative income → subtract prior quarterly payments → subtract 2307 credits → result is the installment due this quarter.
Deadlines: Q1: May 15 · Q2: Aug 15 · Q3: Nov 15
3
Attach SAWT (.dat file) and all 2307s
The SAWT (Summary of Alphalist of Withholding Taxes) is a .dat file generated from your 2307s using BIR's eSubmission software. You must submit it before or with each 1701Q and your annual return.
Steps: Encode all 2307s → generate SAWT → submit via eSubmission portal → attach validation to your return.
4
File annual return (1701A or 1701) — reconcile everything
The annual return covers the full year (including Q4 which has no separate quarterly filing). It reconciles all three quarterly payments and all 2307 credits.
Use 1701A if: purely self-employed, using 8% or OSD.
Use 1701 if: itemized deductions or mixed income.
Deadline: April 15 of the following year.
5
Handle excess CWT credits
If total 2307 credits exceed your annual income tax due, you have excess CWT. Options per NIRC Sec. 76:
(a) Carry over to next taxable year — mark "carry over" on your annual ITR (irrevocable once elected).
(b) Apply for refund — file a written claim within 2 years from payment date.
Use the Regime Calculator to see if your CWT credits will exceed tax due before filing.
Reconciliation Walkthroughs
SAWT & 2307 Reconciliation — Step-by-StepSummary Alphalist of Withholding Taxes · BIR eSubmission · Required for 1701Q and 1701/1701A
▾
What is the SAWT?The SAWT is a machine-readable summary (.dat file) of all BIR Form 2307s you received in a quarter. BIR uses it to cross-match EWT credits with what your clients declared as withheld. A mismatch triggers a Letter of Authority.
1
Collect all BIR Form 2307s for the quarter
Request 2307s from every corporate client or withholding agent who paid you. They must issue within 20 days from close of quarter (e.g., Q1 2307s by April 20). Keep originals — BIR may ask for physical copies during audit.
2
Encode 2307 data into BIR's eSubmission software
Download the Data Entry Module (DEM) from the BIR website. For each 2307 enter: payor TIN, payor name, income payment amount, ATC code, quarter, withheld amount. Save regularly — the DEM is known to crash.
3
Validate and generate the .dat file
Click Validate in the DEM. Fix any errors (wrong TIN format, ATC mismatch). Once validated, generate and save the .dat file. This is your SAWT file.
4
Submit SAWT via BIR eSubmission portal
Go to eSubmission.bir.gov.ph. Upload your .dat SAWT file. Print or save the Validation and Submission Report — this is your proof of SAWT submission. You will attach this to your 1701Q or annual return.
5
Reconcile: SAWT total = 2307 sum = CWT credit claimed
The total withheld per your SAWT must exactly match: (a) the sum of all 2307 physical copies, and (b) the CWT credit you claim on your 1701Q.
Common mismatch causes: missing 2307 from a client, client filed 2307 with wrong amount, client hasn't filed yet. Resolve discrepancies before filing — BIR will reconcile both sides and issue deficiency assessments for discrepancies.
